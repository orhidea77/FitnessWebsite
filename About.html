Together we can do it

During the 19th century women participated in many forms of recreational fitness.
Specific activities depended largely on the culture and social class, but some activities included horse back riding, dancing, tennis, and early versions of badminton. Social, commercial and scientific developments between 1900 and 1920 led to the increase of physical activities available to American women. Opportunities for women flourished as organized sports began to grow in popularity and physical education programs for girls were introduced in schools.
One sport that was introduced in America at the turn of the century was basketball which quickly swept over schools and playgrounds across the nation.
While initially men played the sport, it wasn't long before women too could be found playing basketball at parks, YWCA's, playgrounds, and in schools from kindergarten's through universities.
